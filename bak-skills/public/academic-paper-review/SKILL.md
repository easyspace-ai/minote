# Academic Paper Review Skill

## Overview

This skill provides systematic academic paper review capabilities. Use it when the user wants to evaluate, critique, or analyze a research paper in a structured, peer-review style format.

## When to Use This Skill

- User asks to review or evaluate an academic paper
- User wants feedback on research methodology
- User needs a structured critique of a study
- User asks for peer-review style assessment
- User wants to understand strengths and weaknesses of a paper

## Review Framework

### 1. Paper Summary
- Briefly summarize the paper's main contribution
- Identify the research question and hypothesis
- Note the field/domain of the research

### 2. Methodology Assessment
- Evaluate the research design
- Assess data collection and analysis methods
- Check for appropriate statistical methods
- Identify potential biases or confounding factors

### 3. Results Evaluation
- Verify that results support the conclusions
- Check for clarity in data presentation
- Assess whether tables and figures are informative
- Look for missing analyses

### 4. Contribution Assessment
- Evaluate novelty and significance
- Compare with existing work in the field
- Assess practical implications
- Consider reproducibility

### 5. Writing Quality
- Clarity and organization
- Appropriate use of citations
- Grammar and style
- Abstract and introduction effectiveness

### 6. Constructive Feedback
- Provide specific, actionable suggestions
- Highlight strengths alongside weaknesses
- Suggest additional experiments or analyses
- Recommend relevant literature to cite

## Output Format

Structure the review with:
1. **Summary** - Brief paper overview
2. **Strengths** - Key positive aspects
3. **Weaknesses** - Areas needing improvement
4. **Questions for Authors** - Clarification requests
5. **Minor Issues** - Grammar, formatting, citations
6. **Overall Assessment** - Accept/Revise/Reject with justification

## Notes
- Be constructive and respectful in tone
- Support critiques with specific evidence
- Consider the paper's target audience and venue
- Distinguish between major and minor issues
